## Polylogue: decisions to change

### Supersede `polylogue-6mv`

Current decision: Polylogue owns raw transcripts; Sinex must never ingest them.

Replacement:

> In Sinex-backed deployments, Sinex is the canonical durable material and domain-history backend for complete Polylogue transcript evidence. Polylogue retains ontology, normalization, stable domain identity, logical composition, context/memory behavior, and product surfaces. Raw bodies stay out of generic event payloads and generic MCP responses but live in the protected Sinex material plane.

The superseding Bead should cite the authority, identity, revision, privacy, and rebuild contracts.

### Re-scope `polylogue-fs1.9`

Keep a low-volume emitter for:

- new revision available;
- projection lag/readiness;
- session activity boundary;
- correlation hints;
- redacted notifications;
- health and coverage signals.

Do not treat that event stream as the canonical transcript store.

## Closure rule

A Bead in this legibility cut closes only when its acceptance evidence is attached in one of these forms:

- deterministic proof packet;
- focused test receipt;
- clean-install receipt;
- field packet with independent oracle;
- generated-surface drift check;
- cold-reader/adversarial audit;
- rebuild/parity packet;
- live-stack fault-injection packet.

A screenshot, prose summary, or merged code without the acceptance artifact is not closure evidence.
