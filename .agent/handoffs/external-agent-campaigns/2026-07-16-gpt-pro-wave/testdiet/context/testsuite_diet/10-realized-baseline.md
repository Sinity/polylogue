## Consequences for the five representative clusters

1. **Seeded artifact integrity** adapts the realized workload artifact and
   receipt identities into fail-closed cache publication and immutable cloning.
   It may add independently planted expected facts for a selected canary, but
   it may not invent another semantic workload profile.
2. **Exact query selection** begins with the realized C-03 exact-session
   actions canary and extends it to membership/count/partition/page and
   preview/apply agreement. Its work-bound sensitivity should reuse the C-03
   global-first mutant or an equivalent production mutation.
3. **Convergence restart/retry** selects the realized partial-convergence and
   active-growing variants. It adds the durable restart law, not another
   corpus generator or convergence-state description.
4. **Incremental/rebuild equivalence** replays the same workload identity
   through incremental and rebuild routes and compares independent public
   facts. User overlays remain outside workload/content identity.
5. **Devtools subtraction** consumes the merged seed/testmon receipts as live
   evidence and avoids every file owned by the three `b054` proof children.

## Pre-dispatch reconciliation gate

Before Sol assigns a write file:

1. rebase or otherwise inspect the merged revision containing the authorities
   above;
2. regenerate `census/clusters.json` dossiers and replace snapshot-relative
   symbols, tests, canary paths, and receipt paths with their real names;
3. prove every proposed write file is outside the upstream implementation and
   any active overlapping work;
4. identify the exact existing canary and receipt to reuse in each prompt;
5. delete any planned helper whose entire purpose is now supplied upstream;
6. leave the savings ledger unchanged until a Diet cluster itself merges.

If a named upstream outcome is absent after merge, the dossier is blocked. A
worker does not fill the gap speculatively inside its assigned cluster.
