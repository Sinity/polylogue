## 2. The launch should demonstrate a result before explaining the platform

The recommended public arc remains Receipts ŌåÆ Count It Once ŌåÆ honesty anti-demo, but the dossier tightens the construct and channel logic.

### Act 1: Receipts

Show:

1. what the assistant claimed;
2. what the structured tool result recorded;
3. the exact source ref;
4. a text-only false positive containing words such as ŌĆ£errorŌĆØ without an actual failed tool;
5. a missing-evidence case that refuses certainty.

This is both memorable and falsifiable. It establishes why Polylogue is more than transcript grep without pretending grep is useless.

### Act 2: Count It Once

Show:

- a parent session;
- a copied-prefix fork;
- every physical message still inspectable;
- the unique logical tail;
- a fresh-subagent control that must **not** be deduplicated.

The claim is not ŌĆ£Polylogue removes duplicate files.ŌĆØ It is:

> **Keep every receipt; derive a separate view that does not count inherited work as new work.**

### Act 3: Honesty anti-demo

Show a question that the archive cannot responsibly answer because a source is absent, acquisition is partial, or the requested claim exceeds the evidence.

This is strategically important. Nearly every competitor can show another successful trace. Far fewer lead with proof that the system knows when it may not claim completeness.

## 4. Synthetic provider-shaped fixtures remain defensibleŌĆöwith controls

The legal-risk memo concludes that minimal, clean-room, entirely invented fixtures are a **low-to-moderate practical risk, not zero risk**.

Provider terms contain restrictions involving reverse engineering, extraction, scraping, automated access, replication, or competing use. The exact contractual consequence depends on the account, agreement, acquisition method, and jurisdiction. Copyright and interoperability authorities support bounded arguments, not a blanket right to reproduce any interface or file. ŅłĆciteŅłéturn289079view1Ņłéturn367618view1Ņłéturn289079view3Ņłéturn297842view18Ņłéturn289079view5Ņłü

The proposed fixture policy requires:

- entirely invented human and model content;
- the minimum fields required to test a construct;
- no copied vendor examples, documentation, prompts, output, code, UI, or logos;
- no authentication or encryption bypass;
- an explicit clean-room provenance record;
- observed-format dates;
- parser/fixture versioning;
- a non-affiliation notice;
- repository residue and secret scanning;
- terms rechecks before releases;
- counsel escalation for nonpublic, encrypted, or access-controlled formats.

## 5. The privacy narrative must be uncomfortable to be credible

Public discussion around Rewind, Recall, and ActivityWatch repeatedly returns to:

- a consolidated local archive becoming a high-value target;
- bystander and correspondent consent;
- hidden cloud egress behind ŌĆ£localŌĆØ marketing;
- exclusion and deletion behavior;
- retained derived copies;
- capture gaps and false conclusions;
- employer surveillance;
- whether open source is accompanied by understandable defaults and boundaries.

MicrosoftŌĆÖs present Recall documentation emphasizes local storage, encryption, opt-in behavior, filters, and deletion controls precisely because those are expected questions. ActivityWatch similarly emphasizes local storage while warning that it cannot guarantee security and that remote exposure requires care. ŅłĆciteŅłéturn388789view0Ņłéturn388789view1Ņłéturn137491search0Ņłéturn137491search3Ņłéturn220790search3Ņłéturn220790search7Ņłü

The recommended Polylogue answer is not ŌĆ£private by designŌĆØ:

> Polylogue is not a whole-screen lifelog. It imports supported AI-session evidence that the user selects or explicitly captures. The core archive is local, but local data can still be highly sensitive. Optional networked paths exist. Application-level encryption and complete selective physical forgetting are not general current guarantees, and missing evidence should be surfaced as a caveat rather than interpreted as proof that nothing happened.

# Strategic synthesis

The five greatest risks are:

1. category collision and expectation debt;
2. overstating privacy because storage is local;
3. competing against observability platforms on their strongest ground;
4. provider-format and terms fragility;
5. treating a deterministic demo as proof of general production behavior.

The five immediate no-code wins are:

1. replace the hero copy;
2. publish the honest comparison matrix;
3. lead every channel with Receipts;
4. adopt fixture provenance manifests;
5. publish the uncomfortable privacy FAQ before launch.

See [top risks and free wins](sandbox:/mnt/data/polylogue-category-launch-research/09-strategic-risks-and-free-wins.md).

# Sinex relationship

The dossier preserves the maximal direction without turning it into a current claim:

- Sinex ultimately stores provider-native artifacts, normalized transcript material, durable Polylogue-domain observations, judgments, context delivery, effects, and lifecycle state.
- Polylogue retains provider normalization, AI-work ontology, transcript composition and lineage, memory/context policy, query behavior, and product UX.
- SQLite remains the standalone authority or backed-mode edge projection, cache, local index, and offline outbox.
- The current integration remains materially thinner than that target.

This appears in the claims baseline, message house, executive deck, privacy analysis, and the dedicated Sinex public-story fork.
